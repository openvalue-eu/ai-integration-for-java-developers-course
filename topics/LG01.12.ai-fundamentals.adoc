=== Types of AI
[.notes]
--
**Instructor Notes:** Give a quick rundown of AI categories and comparing them. How the types of AI is grouped by capabilities and functionalities.
--

**Key Points to Cover:**
[%step]
--
- AI by Capabilities:
** Narrow AI: specific tasks (e.g., code completion)
** General AI: human-like intelligence (future goal)
** Superintelligent AI: surpass human intelligence (theoretical)
- AI by Functionalities:
** Reactive Machines (Narrow AI): no memory, respond to current input (e.g., chess AI)
** Limited Memory (General AI): use past data for decisions (e.g., self-driving cars)
** Theory of Mind: understand emotions, beliefs (research stage)
** Self-Aware (Super AI): possess self-consciousness (theoretical)

=== Critical AI Limitations

[.notes]
--
**Instructor Notes:** Emphasize this is most important concept for safe usage. Use examples of confident but incorrect AI suggestions.
--

**Key Points to Cover:**
[%step]
--
- Probabilistic, not logical reasoning
- Can confidently generate buggy/insecure code
- Limited by training data
- No awareness of your production constraints
- Always review and test AI-generated code

=== Discussion: Where Can AI Help?
[.notes]
--
**Instructor Notes:** Connect ML concepts to daily work. Expected insights: AI good at boilerplate, struggles with novel business logic.

**Discussion Prompt:**

**Common Concerns to Address:**
[%step]

- "Is AI just guessing?" - Yes, probabilistic predictions based on learned patterns
- "When should we not use AI?" - Novel problems, security-critical code without review
